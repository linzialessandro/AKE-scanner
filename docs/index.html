AKE Scanner · in-browser lab

Watch a sentence meet the primes

The Ax–Kochen–Ershov principle says that for many first-order sentences φ, truth in the p-adics ℚp for all large primes is equivalent to truth in the Laurent series field 𝔽p((t)). This page runs the real ake-scanner package (via Pyodide) on constructive predicates and shows the asymptotic pattern — exceptional primes, threshold N, clean tail — on a prime strip you can read at a glance.

Lightweight on purpose: system fonts, no framework circus, one heavy dependency (Python in WASM). The interesting part is the math, not the chrome.
